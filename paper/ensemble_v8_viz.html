Fifty possible paths, one storm.

Instead of one forecast line, the model draws many coherent routes by sampling its own cross-lead error distribution — then reports the distribution: an ensemble-mean track, a growing cone of uncertainty, and strike probability. The point estimate never changes; the value is the spread.

How it reads

  1. Sample the error field. Draw noise from the 40-dim (20 leads × 2 axes) forecast-error covariance and add it to v8's per-step motion — correlated across leads, so each route stays smooth.
  2. Integrate to routes. Cumulative-sum each sample into a full 120 h path. Fifty draws → fifty coherent tracks.
  3. Aggregate as a distribution. The mean path is the point forecast; the member spread is the cone; the fraction of routes passing a location is its strike probability — a mean of indicators, not of positions.
  4. Score properly. Rank samplers by the energy score (multivariate CRPS), not by error of the mean.

Which covariance makes the best ensemble?

Same routes, four ways to model the error correlations. Lower energy score is better; 90% coverage should sit near 0.90.

Cross-lead correlation is decisive: the diagonal sampler leaves the truth inside its cone only 20% of the time. Any correlated covariance jumps to ~82% — and your generalized-MP edge wins outright on the proper score.
