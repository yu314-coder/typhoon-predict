One storm it reads, one it can't.

Two real 2020s typhoons the model never trained on, each forecast 120 hours from an early fix. Bavi is a textbook recurving super typhoon — the model tracks it well. Co-may is the strange one: it looped south, then swung far north. Best-track history gives no hint of that, and the forecast fails. Same model, opposite outcomes — the difference is the storm.

Bavi (2026), Cat-5. A well-behaved west-then-recurve-north track. The model holds within ~200–450 km through 120 h — as good as any best-track-only model gets.

Co-may (2025), the strange one. It made a tight loop and reversed direction. The past track the model sees points one way; the storm went another. Error blows past 1,000 km by 48 h. Notably the ensemble spreads wide here — the model's own uncertainty flags that this storm is unpredictable from history alone, even as the mean is wrong.

The honest takeaway: TrackFormer is competitive on typical storms, but erratic, weakly-steered tracks need environmental data it doesn't have. The ensemble at least tells you when to distrust it.
