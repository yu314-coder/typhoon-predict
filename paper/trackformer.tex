\large with a Random-Matrix Block-Structured Uncertainty Head}

\begin{abstract}
We forecast the full state of a tropical cyclone --- storm motion, maximum wind, central pressure,
radius of maximum wind, and the twelve 34/50/64-kt wind-radii --- at twenty six-hourly lead times
(6--120\,h) from best-track history alone. Starting from a single-stream multi-task Transformer, we
document a concrete instance of \emph{negative transfer}: adding physically informative
motion-dynamics features (heading, speed, turn-rate, intensity trends) markedly improves intensity
and structure prediction yet slightly degrades track, and na\"ively up-weighting the track loss makes
track \emph{worse}. A paired storm-level bootstrap shows the track change is statistically
insignificant, isolating the true cause as gradient conflict over shared capacity rather than loss
balance. We then present \textbf{TrackFormer}, a protected dual-stream architecture that routes
kinematic and thermodynamic gradients separately, lets thermodynamics influence track only through a
zero-initialized gated adapter (so intensity tasks can help track but never harm it), and predicts
track as a damped constant-velocity persistence baseline plus a learned recurvature residual.
Finally we derive a block-structured probabilistic head whose track block is a matrix-normal
covariance and whose noise bulk is cleaned by a two-point generalized sample-covariance
Marchenko--Pastur estimator. On held-out post-2020 storms TrackFormer improves track while retaining
the intensity and pressure gains.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem and data}
Let a storm be a time-ordered sequence of best-track fixes. From each fix $t_0$ we form a
\emph{window}: a history of $H{=}9$ steps at $6$-h spacing ending at $t_0$, and a target of
$L{=}20$ future states at leads $\{6,12,\dots,120\}$\,h. Each future state is the $17$-vector
\begin{equation}
\bm y_\ell = \big(\underbrace{\Delta e_\ell,\ \Delta n_\ell}_{\text{per-step motion (km)}},\
v^{\max}_\ell,\ p_\ell,\ r^{\mathrm{mw}}_\ell,\ \underbrace{r^{34}_{\text{ne..nw}},r^{50}_{\cdot},r^{64}_{\cdot}}_{\text{12 wind radii}}\big),
\qquad \ell = 1,\dots,20 .
\end{equation}
Track error is the RMS great-circle-plane distance between the predicted and observed cumulative
displacement, $\mathrm{cumsum}_\ell(\Delta e,\Delta n)$; the other four groups are reported as masked
mean-absolute error (labels are frequently missing, especially pre-satellite and for wind radii).

\paragraph{Dataset.} We use IBTrACS v04r01 (all basins, 1980+), yielding $84{,}150$ windows. We split
by storm to prevent leakage: train ($\le 2015$), validation ($2016$--$2019$), test ($\ge 2020$).
Inputs are standardized per feature using \emph{training-split statistics only}. Missing measurements
are zero-filled with explicit presence-flag channels (informative sparsity) rather than
mean-imputed.

\paragraph{Motion-dynamics features.} Beyond the original $40$ features (motion vectors, intensity,
radii, seasonality, validity), we add $8$ that make the storm's instantaneous dynamics explicit:
current heading $(\sin\theta,\cos\theta)$, speed, signed turn-rate (recurvature), and per-step
intensity/pressure trends with their validity flags, for a $48$-dimensional history feature per step.
Their training-set means are physically sensible: mean heading points west-northwest (the trade-wind
easterlies), mean speed $\approx 14$\,km\,h$^{-1}$, and mean intensification $+1.5$\,kt per step.

\section{The negative-transfer diagnosis}
A single-stream model (linear projection $\to$ 4-layer encoder $\to$ 6-layer lead-query decoder $\to$
dual state / log-scale heads, diagonal Gaussian NLL + masked Huber) trained on the $40$-feature set
reaches the ``old'' row of Table~\ref{tab:results}. Adding the $8$ motion-dynamics features (``v2'')
\emph{improves} vmax, pressure, RMW, and radius substantially (pressure MAE $\oldWPpres\to\vtwoWPpres$
hPa on WP-2020+) but \emph{raises} track error ($\oldWPtrack\to\vtwoWPtrack$\,km). Up-weighting the
track loss $\times 3$ made track \emph{worse still} ($806.6$\,km), the signature of gradient conflict:
a larger track coefficient destabilizes the shared optimization rather than reallocating capacity.

\paragraph{Is the regression even real?} We paired-bootstrap the WP-2020+ per-window track errors by
resampling \emph{storms} (not overlapping windows), the correct unit of independence.
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\Delta}_{\text{track}} = \text{mean}_{\text{storms}}\big(e^{\text{v2}} - e^{\text{old}}\big)
= +17.7~\text{km},\qquad \text{95\% CI } [-22.2,\ +60.5]~\text{km},\qquad \Pr(\text{v2 worse})=0.79 .
\end{equation}
The interval spans zero over the $103$ WP test storms: track is \emph{statistically tied}, while the
intensity gains are large and consistent. The features are not the problem; the shared representation
is. This motivates an architecture that protects the kinematic pathway.

\paragraph{Dual encoders.} The $48$ features are split into a kinematic view
$\bm x^{\text{kin}}$ (motion, heading, speed, turn, seasonality, lead offset; $11$ dims) and a
thermodynamic view $\bm x^{\text{th}}$ (vmax, pressure, gust, RMW, $12$ radii, trend features, $16$
validity flags; $36$ dims). Each is projected to $d{=}256$, given a learned temporal embedding, and
encoded by a pre-norm Transformer ($4$ and $3$ layers respectively, $8$ heads).

\paragraph{Protected gradient routing.} Let $\bm H^{\text{kin}},\bm H^{\text{th}}\in\mathbb R^{9\times d}$
be the encoder outputs. The track decoder cross-attends to $\bm H^{\text{kin}}$; the intensity decoder
cross-attends to $[\,\bm H^{\text{th}};\ \mathrm{sg}(\bm H^{\text{kin}})\,]$ where $\mathrm{sg}(\cdot)$
is stop-gradient. Thus motion still \emph{informs} intensity, but intensity gradients cannot corrupt
the kinematic encoder. Symmetrically, thermodynamics reaches the track head only through
\begin{equation}
\bm H^{\text{track}}_\ell \;=\; \mathrm{TrackDec}(\bm q_\ell,\bm H^{\text{kin}})
\;+\; \alpha_\ell\,\mathcal A\!\big(\mathrm{sg}(\bar{\bm H}^{\text{th}})\big),
\qquad \alpha_\ell \overset{\text{init}}{=} 0,
\label{eq:gate}
\end{equation}
where $\bar{\bm H}^{\text{th}}$ is the mean-pooled thermodynamic context, $\mathcal A$ is a
bottleneck-$64$ adapter with a zero-initialized output layer, and $\alpha_\ell$ is a per-lead scalar.
Because $\alpha_\ell$ and $\mathcal A$ start at zero and receive gradient only from the track loss, the
intensity branch can \emph{help} track (if it lowers track error) but can never harm it: at
initialization TrackFormer reproduces a protected track-only model exactly.

\paragraph{Persistence-residual track head.} Rather than regress $20$ free displacement vectors, we
anchor track on a damped constant-velocity baseline and predict only the deviation:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\Delta \bm p}_\ell \;=\; \underbrace{\rho_\ell\,\bm v_0}_{\text{damped persistence}}
\;+\; \underbrace{\bm r_\ell(\bm H^{\text{track}}_\ell)}_{\text{learned recurvature residual}},
\qquad \bm v_0=\text{current 6-h motion (km)},
\end{equation}
with a learned per-lead damping schedule $\rho_\ell$ (initialized to $1$) and a zero-initialized
residual head $\bm r_\ell$. The network therefore begins as pure persistence --- a strong physical
prior --- and learns only recurvature and along/cross-track acceleration.

\paragraph{Losses.} Track uses a lead-weighted ($w_\ell=\sqrt\ell$, mean-normalized) Huber on per-step
motion plus a cumulative-position Huber; it is never down-weighted by a predicted variance. Intensity
uses masked Huber $+$ Gaussian NLL with a monotone wind-radii penalty $\mathrm{ReLU}(r^{50}-r^{34})+
\mathrm{ReLU}(r^{64}-r^{50})$.

\section{Random-matrix block-structured uncertainty head}
Operational value requires calibrated \emph{joint} uncertainty across the $340$ outputs
($20$ leads $\times 17$ variables). A full $340\times340$ covariance is both statistically
ill-posed (few independent storms, overlapping windows, heavy masking) and architecturally harmful:
it would re-introduce a track$\leftrightarrow$thermodynamics coupling and undo the protection above.
We therefore impose a \emph{block-diagonal} covariance
\begin{equation}
\bm\Sigma \;=\; \mathrm{blkdiag}\big(\bm\Sigma_{\text{track}},\ \bm\Sigma_{\text{int}},\ \bm\Sigma_{\text{rad}}\big),
\end{equation}
with a matrix-normal track block $\bm\Sigma_{\text{track}} = \bm K_{\text{lead}}\otimes\bm K_{\text{axis}}
+ \mathrm{diag}(\bm s^2)$ ($\bm K_{\text{lead}}$ a learned $20\times20$ temporal kernel, $\bm K_{\text{axis}}$
a $2\times2$ along/cross-track kernel) and low-rank$+$diagonal thermodynamic blocks
$\bm\Sigma_\bullet=\bm D\bm D^\top+\mathrm{diag}(\bm s^2)$, $\bm D\in\mathbb R^{p\times r}$, $r{=}8$--$16$.

\paragraph{Spectral cleaning.} Sample error-covariance eigenvalues mix a few genuine ``signal''
spikes with a wide noise bulk. Under the two-point generalized sample-covariance model, the
companion Stieltjes transform of the limiting spectral law solves a cubic
\begin{equation}
a z\,m^3 + \big[a(z-y+1)+z\big]m^2 + \big[a+z-y+1-y\beta(a-1)\big]m + 1 = 0,
\label{eq:cubic}
\end{equation}
where $y=p/n$ is the aspect ratio and $(a,\beta)$ parameterize a two-population noise structure. The
support edges of the noise bulk are the $z$ at which \eqref{eq:cubic} loses a real root (a conjugate
pair appears); eigenvalues above the upper edge $\lambda_+$ are retained as signal and the bulk is
replaced by a positive floor:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\bm\Sigma} = \bm U\,\mathrm{diag}\!\big(\lambda_i\,\mathbb 1[\lambda_i>\lambda_+]
 + \bar\lambda_{\text{bulk}}\,\mathbb 1[\lambda_i\le\lambda_+]\big)\,\bm U^\top .
\end{equation}
Setting $a{=}1$ recovers the classical Marchenko--Pastur edge $\lambda_+=(1+\sqrt y)^2$. The estimator
is fit on \emph{independent storm-block} residuals and validated out-of-sample; it is applied
\emph{per block} and only after the mean heads have converged, so it sharpens calibration without
perturbing the point forecast.

TrackFormer lowers WP-2020+ track error from $\oldWPtrack$ to $659.0$\,km ($-8.5\%$) and all-basin
from $\oldABtrack$ to $592.1$\,km, while simultaneously \emph{improving} pressure, vmax, and wind-radius
MAE. The same paired storm-level bootstrap that found the v2 change insignificant now confirms the v3
gain is real: $\widehat\Delta_{\text{track}}=-59.9$\,km, 95\% CI $[-103.2,\,-16.0]$\,km,
$\Pr(\text{v3 better})=0.995$. Removing the negative transfer --- rather than adding parameters or
tuning loss weights --- is what breaks the previous track floor.

\paragraph{Data, not architecture, drove the final gain.} With the architecture fixed, we enlarged
the training set by keeping \emph{partial-lead} windows --- storm-end and short-storm fixes that lack
a full 120\,h future are retained with their missing leads masked rather than discarded --- which
doubled the clean 1980+ training windows ($84$k$\,\to\,153$k). The resulting model (v8) is best on
\emph{every} metric of Table~\ref{tab:results}: WP-2020+ track $\vthreeWPtrack\,\to\,648.8$\,km and
simultaneous improvements in wind, pressure, RMW, and radius. (Extending further to pre-1980 storms
helped track marginally but degraded intensity, because pre-satellite intensity labels are
unreliable; masking those labels while keeping the older \emph{positions} recovered the intensity but
not a significant track gain --- confirming track is information-limited, not data-quantity-limited.)

\paragraph{Position-awareness is the largest lever within IBTrACS.} The dual-stream model is
\emph{translation-invariant} --- it sees only relative motion, never where the storm actually is ---
so it cannot use latitude, which governs the Coriolis force, the climatological recurvature band, and
(through the seasonal thermal structure) sea-surface temperature. Adding a third \emph{environmental}
stream of IBTrACS-derived features (absolute latitude/longitude, distance-to-land, and a latitude$+$month
climatological SST proxy), routed to the track decoder for steering climatology and to the intensity
decoder for the SST signal, cut WP-2020+ track from $\veightWPtrack$ to $618.4$\,km and all-basin from
$\veightABtrack$ to $543.4$\,km, with a $\sim$2-kt wind improvement --- all without any reanalysis
download, so the model stays deployable from a best-track CSV. On out-of-sample case studies it
\emph{halved} the error on an erratic recurving storm (Co-may 2025) and improved a well-behaved
super typhoon (Bavi 2026); only a pathological multi-loop storm (Wayne 1986) regressed, where
climatology cannot substitute for the real-time steering flow.

\section{Discussion}
The consistent lesson across experiments is \textbf{data diversity $>$ engineered features $>$
parameters}: a $17.7$M single-stream model overfit and did worse than a $3.3$M one, and a track-only
model matches an ERA5-fed model, so raw parameter count is not the lever. Track error is bounded below
by \emph{information}, not capacity: five-day recurvature is governed by the evolving large-scale
steering flow, which best-track history cannot fully reveal. The clearest path past the present floor
is a compact environmental-steering branch (deep-layer winds, 500-hPa geopotential) fused
\emph{strongly to track, weakly to intensity} --- the natural next stream in the protected design.
Within history-only inputs, TrackFormer's contribution is architectural: it removes the negative
transfer that a single stream suffers, so intensity and structure gains no longer cost track accuracy.
