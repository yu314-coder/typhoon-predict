Coastlines are Natural Earth 50 m land polygons, simplified to the resolution each panel can actually show. Projection is equirectangular with longitude scaled by cos(latitude), so shapes are locally true at the centre of each panel.

Everything drawn here is a forecast. The bold line is the +120 h track: at each moment, the position predicted by the forecast launched exactly 120 hours earlier. One member, no averaging. The dashed line combines every forecast launched at least 48 hours before the moment it describes, weighted minimum-variance on a Marchenko-Pastur-cleaned lead×lead covariance fitted on other storms.

Neither line ever uses a launch newer than its stated horizon, so both errors are directly comparable to a forecast error at that horizon. The per-valid-time consensus shown on the other maps is not comparable and is not drawn here: it leans on initialisations only six hours old, which is why it scores near 100 km beside a 1250 km five-day error. No such product exists — at 12:00 you cannot use the 06:00 run to say where the storm is at 12:00.

The observed track is reconstructed from the base position of every window, which is an actual best-track fix, so it is data rather than a fit.
