Haiyan, forecast from its first Category-4 hour.

A single 120-hour forecast issued at Super Typhoon Haiyan's first Cat-4 fix (2013-11-05 21:00 UTC, 120 kt). The model draws the mean track and a 50-member ensemble; the amber line is what the storm actually did. It captures the early motion, then — like any best-track-only model — under-runs Haiyan's exceptional speed and misses the late recurvature toward Vietnam.

What the map shows

First 24 h: excellent. The forecast mean sits almost on the observed position — the model reads the west-northwest heading and speed well while the storm is over open ocean.

Then it lags. Haiyan was one of the fastest-moving intense typhoons on record and later recurved north. The best-track-only model can't see the steering flow driving that, so its track falls behind and south of the real path — the error grows with lead time.

The ensemble is honest about it. The spread widens downstream, and the northern members do reach toward the observed recurvature — the uncertainty cone covers the failure the mean can't.
