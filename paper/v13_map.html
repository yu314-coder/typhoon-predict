What the map proves. Every attempt to fix direction from best-track data alone failed — curved baselines, explicit direction losses, bias calibration. v13 is the first version given the surrounding pressure field, and it is better at every lead on Wayne, Co-may and Hinnamnor: Wayne's 120-h error falls 1236 → 873 km, Co-may 488 → 325, Hinnamnor 938 → 806.

Information, not architecture, was the ceiling. The pressure pattern over China, Indochina, Taiwan and the Philippine Sea is what steers a storm — and it downloads in seconds. The gain is biggest exactly where the storms are hardest, which is the signature of adding real physics rather than tuning a loss.
